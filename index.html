How to Use
What is this?
Mixed Up Rhythms is a rhythm practice tool for working musicians and serious students. Enter a melodic passage from a piece you are working on, and the app generates that passage written in every rhythm pattern for a given note grouping — a complete systematic workout in all rhythms.
Step 1 — Choose a note grouping
Choose a note grouping by tapping one of the six buttons above.
Step 2 — Set clef, key, and instrument
Choose the clef your instrument reads in and the key of your passage. The instrument setting transposes the audio preview to concert pitch — so if you play B♭ clarinet, what you hear matches what you sound.
Step 3 — Name and enter your passage
Type a document name, then tap notes on the keyboard. Tap a chip's spelling button to cycle enharmonics. Use ► Play Passage to hear your entry back.
Step 4 — Generate
Tap Generate Exercises to produce the full set of rhythm patterns.
Step 5 — Save & explore
Tap ▲ Save Exercise to save your exercise to your personal library. You'll be asked for your name, email, and instrument once — the app remembers you after that. Use ☰ Library to browse your saved exercises or search the community library by instrument or composer.
Tips
— Your passage plays once through each pattern, stopping when all notes are used.
— Any passage length works — a few notes or an entire phrase.
— Tap ↻ Start Over to reset and begin a new passage.
